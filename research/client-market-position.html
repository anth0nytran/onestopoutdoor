What Separates Page 1 from Everyone Else

The 5 Things the Top Companies All Do

We studied every top-ranking site in your market. The companies on page 1 of Google all share these 5 characteristics. The companies on page 2 and beyond are missing most of them.

1

They Stack Trust Signals — Not Just One, But 4-5 at Once

The elite companies show their Google review count, named testimonials, third-party badges (BBB, HomeAdvisor, Houzz), years in business, and a visual step-by-step process — all on the same homepage. Average companies show maybe one or two of these. The more trust signals layered together, the faster a visitor converts from "just browsing" to calling.

2

They Make It Ridiculously Easy to Contact Them

Top sites show their phone number 4-6 times per page. They have a contact form in the hero section AND at the bottom. They use varied language — "Get a Quote," "Free Consultation," "Start Your Quote," "Call Now." Average sites bury one phone number in the header and call it a day. The easier you make it to call, the more calls you get.

3

They Have a Page for Every City They Serve

Lone Star Patio Builders has 15+ individual city pages. When someone searches "patio cover Katy TX," they have a page titled exactly that. This is the biggest SEO tactic in your market. Companies without city pages barely show up in local results outside their home city.

4

They Post Fresh Content Regularly

The top 3 companies all maintain active blogs, post on Google Business weekly, and add new project photos to their sites. Google sees them as active, relevant businesses. Stale websites get pushed down.

5

They Use Aspirational, Transformation-Focused Language

The elite companies don't say "we build patios." They say "Turn your patio into a custom outdoor oasis." They sell the outcome — weekend barbecues, family memories, a personal retreat — not just the service. This is the difference between a premium brand and a generic contractor site.

Why this matters for you: These 5 patterns aren't a mystery. They're a checklist. Your website is being built with every single one of these baked in from day one — which puts you ahead of 7 out of 10 companies in your market before you even start ranking.
