Section 05

Actionable Takeaways for Rebrand

Immediate Priority Items

1

Review Generation Engine

Tradition has 115, Lone Star has 244+. Getting to 50+ Google reviews at 4.8+ stars is non-negotiable to compete. Build a post-project review request system.

2

Homepage Structure Must Include

  • Hero with transformation headline + dual CTA (Portfolio + Quote)
  • Embedded lead form in hero section (Name, Phone, Zip, Message)
  • Services grid with icons (6-7 services)
  • Process visualization (4-5 steps)
  • Trust bar (review count, years in business, badges)
  • Testimonials carousel with real names
  • Project photo gallery (12+ photos minimum)
  • Service area map/list
  • Second contact form + phone number at bottom
  • Financing information
3

City Landing Pages

Create dedicated pages for: Richmond TX, Katy TX, Sugar Land TX, Houston TX, Fulshear TX, Rosenberg TX, Missouri City TX, Cypress TX. Each with unique H1, local content, and nearby project examples.

4

Trust Signal Stack

  • Google review badge with count
  • BBB accreditation (apply if not already)
  • HomeAdvisor or Angi badge
  • "X years of experience" prominently displayed
  • Named financing partner(s)
  • License/insurance statements
  • HOA compliance and windstorm certification mentions
5

Content Engine

  • Blog with 2-4 posts/month
  • Project case studies with city names in titles
  • FAQ section (6-10 questions targeting search queries)
  • Video content (project walkthroughs, client testimonials)

Competitive Gaps to Exploit

GapOpportunity
No competitor has a strong before/after comparison toolBuild interactive comparison sliders for project showcases
Only 1 competitor has AI chatImplement AI-powered chat for instant engagement
No competitor shows real-time project timelinesDifferentiate with "Track Your Project" feature
Most competitors lack concrete pricing guidanceFinancing calculator + price range indicators
Few competitors have video contentShort-form video (TikTok/Reels) + YouTube walkthroughs
Only OTD collects budget info in lead formAdd budget field to qualify leads and improve close rate
No competitor targets "concrete" as primary keywordOpportunity to own "concrete contractor" searches for patio slabs

Recommended Color Direction

The market clusters around two color strategies:

  • Dark/premium (TCP, OTD): Charcoal + gold/amber — positions as luxury
  • Natural/trust (Tradition, YGO): Greens + earth tones — positions as outdoor experts

Recommendation: A warm, earthy palette that bridges both — deep forest/sage tones with warm gold accents and clean white space. This avoids the generic "contractor blue" that none of the top competitors use.

Recommended Typography Direction

  • Headlines: Bold, modern sans-serif
  • Body: Clean, readable sans-serif
  • Key insight: TCP stands out with editorial/magazine-quality typography — investing in distinctive type choices pays dividends in perceived quality
