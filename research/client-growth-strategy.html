Your Part

What You Need to Do to Make This Work

We build the website, set up your GBP, write the blog posts, and handle the technical SEO. But there are things only you can do — and they make or break whether this works.

Post on Your Google Business Profile Every Week

This is the single most impactful thing you can do yourself. Google rewards businesses that post regularly on their profile — and most of your competitors don't do this consistently. It's a wide open advantage.

Aim for 1-2 posts per week. They don't need to be perfect. A photo from a job site + a few sentences is all it takes. Here are the types of posts to rotate through:

Post Type 1: Project Showcase (Your Best Post Type)

1-2x per week

Take a photo of a finished (or in-progress) project. Write 2-3 sentences about what you did and where. Always mention the city. Always end with your phone number or "call for a free estimate."

Example Post
"Just wrapped up this 20x16 insulated patio cover in Richmond, TX. The homeowner wanted shade for their back patio and we delivered with recessed LED lighting and a ceiling fan. Thinking about a patio cover? Call us for a free estimate: (832) 945-8084"
Example Post
"Another stamped concrete driveway done in Katy, TX! This homeowner went with our Ashlar Slate pattern in desert tan. Concrete driveways and patios are one of our most popular services. Call today for your free quote."
Example Post
"Before & after on this Sugar Land backyard. Went from plain grass to a full outdoor kitchen with granite counters and a built-in grill. 15+ years of experience in every project. (832) 945-8084"

Post Type 2: Seasonal / Timely

1-2x per month

Tie your services to the time of year. Spring and summer are your biggest seasons — get ahead of it.

Example Post
"Spring is here and outdoor living season is starting in Texas! Now is the time to book your patio cover or outdoor kitchen project before the summer rush. Free estimates for homeowners in Richmond, Katy & Sugar Land. (832) 945-8084"
Example Post
"Storm season is coming. Is your patio cover solid? We build windstorm-ready patio covers and do inspections and repairs. Licensed, insured, 15+ years. Call us today."

Post Type 3: Trust Builder

1-2x per month

Remind people why they should hire you. Your experience, your quality, your repeat customers.

Example Post
"15+ years building outdoor spaces in Richmond and the Houston area. 70% of our work comes from repeat customers and referrals. That says everything about how we treat our clients. Licensed, insured, and ready to work. (832) 945-8084"
Example Post
"Thank you to the Martinez family in Cinco Ranch for the 5-star review! We love building outdoor kitchens for families who love to cook and entertain. If you're thinking about an outdoor kitchen, give us a call for a free estimate."
Why this matters: Google Business posts show up directly in search results when people find your profile. They also signal to Google that your business is active, which improves your local ranking. The competitors who rank highest (Tradition, Lone Star) keep their profiles active. The ones that don't (HHI, Infinite, SL Builders) sit on page 2.

Quick rules for every post:

  • Always include a photo (phone photos are fine — real beats polished)
  • Always mention the city or neighborhood (Richmond, Katy, Sugar Land, Cinco Ranch, etc.)
  • Always mention the service (patio cover, concrete, outdoor kitchen, etc.)
  • Always end with your phone number or "call for a free estimate"
  • Keep it 2-4 sentences. Nobody reads a novel on Google.

Ask for Reviews After Every Job

Your review gap is the biggest thing holding you back. You have 30+ reviews at 5.0 stars — the quality is perfect. But Lone Star has 244+ and Tradition has 115. Google's local ranking algorithm weighs review count AND how recently they were posted.

We'll set up a review follow-up system that makes this easy — a simple link you text or email to the customer after a job. But you need to actually send it.

How to ask (it's simpler than you think)

After a job is done and the customer is happy, send them a text like:

"Hey [Name], thanks again for trusting us with your [patio cover / driveway / etc]. If you're happy with the work, a Google review would really help us out. Here's the link: [your Google review link]"

That's it. No follow-up needed. Most happy customers will do it if you make it easy.

Goal: 5-8 new reviews per month. At that rate you hit 80+ in 6 months and 130+ in 12 months — that puts you past Tradition.

Respond to Every Google Review

When a customer leaves a review, reply within a day or two. Doesn't need to be long. Google tracks whether businesses respond — and customers read the responses.

Response Template
"Thank you [Name]! It was great working on your [project type] in [city]. We appreciate you trusting us with your backyard. Enjoy it!"
Response Template
"Thanks so much [Name]! Projects like this are why we love what we do. If you ever need anything in the future, don't hesitate to call."

Send Us Project Photos

Every time you finish a project, snap a few photos on your phone and text or email them to us. Include what you did and the city/neighborhood. We use these for:

  • Updating your website gallery
  • Blog post content
  • Your monthly content updates

Takes 2 minutes. Before, during, and after shots are best. Phone photos are fine.

Answer Leads Fast

When a quote request comes in from the website, you'll get an email with the customer's name, phone number, service needed, and timeline. Call them back within an hour if you can. Businesses that respond within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to close. Your competitors are not fast at this — you can win just by picking up the phone.
